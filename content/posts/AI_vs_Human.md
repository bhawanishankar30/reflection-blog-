This blog is based on group 3 presentations. Humans make machines and design all the functions of it, how it works and make our work easy. But a question is raised: can machines think like humans ? It is not an easy question and there is no definition of thinking. Nowadays machines are replacing humans from their work and increasing unemployment. It replaced physical work but now there is time for machines to replace the human brain. It can be possible in the future.

They explained about the Turing Test. There is a person, Alan Turing who worked to solve this problem, can machines think as human. His test is based on the comparing answer of human and machine. If we can not differentiate the answer of machine and human it proves that machine is successful .

This test helped to develop more advanced AI technology today. The GPT-4 is more humanized and able to talk like a human. We can not easily identify that the other person is a machine or  AI. But it's not mean that machines are thinking and that they have emotions. Basically it works and replies to us on the basis of its collected data.There are limitations also in turing test the conversation time is too short and not purely dependent on the decision of the judge.

It proves that machines can easily manipulate humans by emotion and the way of giving answers to questions.

The presentation helped me to understand the difference between the human brain and Ai. It was not only informative but also forced me to think about the future generation of AI, that will be more powerful as compared to today's AI machine. It also gives an idea to future AI developers to develop with keeping in mind how AI should be, solve problems well structured and replace the human brain in future.
